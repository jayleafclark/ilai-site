Video editing for beginners comes down to ten skills, learned in order: cutting silence, trimming clips, adding text, syncing to a beat, color correction, audio leveling, transitions, b-roll layering, captions, and exporting for platform. Master these ten in sequence and you can produce a clean 60-second video in under an hour. Everything else is refinement.

Most people who try to learn editing open software first and get overwhelmed by 400 buttons. The better path is to learn one edit at a time, on a real clip, and build a habit before you add complexity. Here's the order that works, based on what actually gets a video watched to the end.

## The first 10 edits, in order

### 1. Cut the dead air
Open any raw clip and remove the pauses, the "ums," and the false starts. This alone can shrink a 3-minute ramble into 90 seconds of usable footage. Do this before anything else.

### 2. Trim your first and last three seconds
The first three seconds decide if someone stays. The last three seconds decide if they remember you. Trim both until they're sharp, not soft.

### 3. Add one line of text per key point
Not subtitles yet, just a headline overlay for your main claim. One sentence, on screen, timed to when you say it out loud.

### 4. Sync a cut to your voice, not the music
Beginners cut on the beat because it looks impressive. Cut on your own speech rhythm first. It reads as more confident and it's easier to control.

### 5. Fix exposure and white balance
This is basic color correction, not a full grade. Pull up the shadows if your face is dark, cool down the tint if it's orange. Two sliders, thirty seconds.

### 6. Level your audio
Aim for around -14 LUFS for social platforms. If you don't have a meter, just make sure your voice doesn't dip lower or spike louder than a normal conversation.

### 7. Use one transition type, consistently
Pick a hard cut, a whip pan, or a crossfade, and use only that one for an entire project. Switching styles mid-video is the fastest way to look amateur.

### 8. Layer in one piece of b-roll
Just one. A screen recording, a product shot, a reaction clip. It breaks up a talking head and gives the eye somewhere else to rest.

### 9. Add captions
Not optional. Roughly 75 to 85% of short-form video gets watched with sound off at some point. Auto-generated captions get you 90% there; you still need to check them for errors before posting.

### 10. Export at the right spec
Vertical for Reels and TikTok (1080x1920), square or vertical for feed posts, horizontal only for YouTube long-form. Exporting at the wrong ratio is the single most common beginner mistake, and it's invisible until the platform crops your subject out of frame.

## Which editing software should a beginner start with?
Start with whatever your phone can run without lag. CapCut is free, mobile-first, and covers edits 1 through 9 on this list without a learning curve. Once you're editing longer-form content or need more control over layers and grading, move to DaVinci Resolve (also free) or Premiere Pro. Don't buy software before you've done ten edits by hand. The tool isn't what's slowing you down at the start.

> The fastest way to look like an amateur editor isn't a bad cut. It's an inconsistent one.

## How long should your first ten edits take?
Realistically, expect your first video to take three to four hours for something that will eventually take twenty minutes. That's normal. Editing speed comes from repetition, not talent. By your tenth video, most creators cut editing time by half just from muscle memory with their own software's shortcuts.

### A rough beginner timeline
- Videos 1-3: expect to redo steps multiple times, especially audio leveling and captions
- Videos 4-6: you'll have a repeatable order of operations
- Videos 7-10: your biggest gains come from tightening pacing, not learning new tools

If you get to video ten and you're still spending four-plus hours per edit, the bottleneck is usually the b-roll or caption step. Those are the two most time-consuming and the two most commonly outsourced once a creator's output picks up.

## FAQ

### Do I need expensive software to start editing video?
No. CapCut and DaVinci Resolve are both free and cover every skill a beginner needs, including color correction, captions, and multi-track audio.

### How many videos does it take to get fast at editing?
Most creators see a real drop in editing time by their tenth video, once the ten core edits become routine instead of a decision each time.

### What's the most common beginner mistake?
Exporting at the wrong aspect ratio for the platform, followed closely by inconsistent transitions across a single video.
